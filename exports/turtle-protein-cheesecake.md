# Turtle Protein Cheesecake 🐢

*Derived from the Vanilla Pumpkin Protein base. Pecan skipped — caramel + chocolate carry the turtle. Dense-tuned base (no water bath, AP flour, less pumpkin). Bake in a **9-inch pan** with a parchment sling.*

| | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Fiber |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Per slice** (1 of 8) | **215** | **18.9 g** | 17.6 g | 7.6 g | 1.8 g |
| Whole cake | 1722 | 151 g | 141 g | 61 g | 15 g |

**Ratio: 11.4 cal per gram of protein.** Splenda throughout, never monk fruit. The brown-sugar caramel is the one honest-sugar indulgence — keep it to a thin drizzle.

---

## Ingredients

**Crust**
- Graham cracker crumbs — 57 g
- Unsweetened cocoa powder — 6 g
- Egg white — 30 g
- Melted butter — 7 g
- Granulated Splenda — 1.5 tbsp
- Salt — 1 pinch

**Filling**
- Pumpkin purée — 75 g
- Cottage cheese — 57 g (blended smooth)
- Light cream cheese — 113 g (softened)
- 0% Greek yogurt — 240 g
- Vanilla protein powder — 2.5 scoops
- Granulated Splenda — 5 tbsp
- Whole eggs — 2
- Egg white — 30 g
- Vanilla extract — 1 tsp
- Lemon juice — 1/3 tsp
- Vanilla SF pudding mix — 2 tbsp
- All-purpose flour — 2 tbsp

**Protein Caramel Swirl** (marbled into filling)
- 0% Greek yogurt — 120 g
- Butterscotch SF pudding mix — 2 tbsp
- Granulated Splenda — 1 tbsp
- Unsweetened almond milk — 1 tbsp
- Salt — 1 pinch

**Real Brown-Sugar Caramel** (top drizzle)
- Brown sugar — 20 g
- Butter — 6 g
- Unsweetened almond milk — 1 tbsp
- Salt — 1 pinch

**Dark-Chocolate Ganache** (top drizzle)
- Unsweetened baking chocolate — 14 g
- Granulated Splenda — 1 tbsp
- Unsweetened almond milk — 1 tbsp (warm)
- Salt — 1 pinch

*Plus flaky salt to finish.*

---

## Method

### 1. Crust
Stir the graham crumbs, cocoa, Splenda, and salt together. Work in the melted butter and egg white until it clumps like wet sand, then press firmly into the bottom of a parchment-lined 9-inch pan — **run the parchment up two opposite sides as a sling** so you can lift the whole cake out later. Bake at **350°F for ~10 min**, then cool while you finish the filling.

### 2. Cook the pumpkin
Cook the pumpkin in a nonstick pan over medium heat, 3–5 min, stirring, until thickened and nutty. Remove and **cool completely** (hot mix will scramble the eggs).

### 3. Blend the cottage cheese
Blend the cottage cheese completely smooth (immersion blender or food processor) — no visible curd, or the cheesecake turns grainy.

### 4. Build the filling
Beat the light cream cheese alone until smooth, then add yogurt, blended cottage cheese, protein powder, Splenda, vanilla, lemon, and vanilla pudding mix. Sift/whisk in the AP flour smooth (no lumps) and mix **just until combined** — don't overwork it. (Warm the bowl ~15 sec if it clumps.)

### 5. Add eggs + pumpkin
Add the 2 whole eggs, the 30 g egg white, and the cooled pumpkin. Mix just until smooth — **do NOT overbeat** once eggs are in (air = cracks, and we want a dense set anyway).

### 6. Make the protein caramel swirl
Whisk the 120 g Greek yogurt, butterscotch pudding mix, Splenda, almond milk, and salt smooth. It should be thick and spoonable like a loose caramel — chill 10 min if you want it firmer to swirl.

### 7. Pour + marble
Pour **half** the filling onto the cooled crust. Drop spoonfuls of the protein caramel swirl over it, add the rest of the filling, then drag a knife through in a few S-strokes to marble (don't overmix). Tap the pan on the counter to level and pop bubbles.

### 8. Bake
Bake at **325–350°F for ~40–50 min — NO water bath**; a dry bake sets it denser. Pull when the center is **just set with only a slight wobble (~155–160°F center)**, not visibly jiggly. It firms further as it cools — do not chase a fully dry center, that's where a lean cheesecake turns rubbery.

### 9. Cool + chill
Oven off, door cracked 10–20 min → counter to room temp ~1–1.5 hr (max 2 hr out) → fridge **at least 4 hr, overnight is best** for the densest, firmest set. Best the next day.

### 10. Make the toppings (day of serving)
- **Real caramel:** Melt the 6 g butter in a small saucepan, add the brown sugar and salt, cook over medium, stirring, until it bubbles and darkens slightly (~2–3 min). Off heat, whisk in the almond milk (it will sputter) until smooth. Cools to thicken; rewarm to drizzle.
- **Ganache:** Melt the baking chocolate gently (microwave in 15-sec bursts). Whisk in the Splenda and the **warm** almond milk a little at a time until glossy and pourable (cold milk can seize it). Use warm.

### 11. Finish
Lift the chilled cake out with the parchment sling onto a plate. Warm the caramel and ganache separately just until pourable — a few seconds; they should **ribbon, not run**. Drizzle both over the top in a back-and-forth criss-cross (spoon, or a zip-top bag with a tiny corner snipped). Finish with a pinch of **flaky salt**. Slice with a hot, wiped knife.
